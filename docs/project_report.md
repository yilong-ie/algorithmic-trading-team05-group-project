# Dynamic Asset Allocation Competition Notes

## Objective

The objective is to maximize the June 2026 live competition performance while
strictly satisfying the project rules:

- only ACWI, AGG, GLD, and BSV are used;
- weights are long-only and sum to 100%;
- every weekly CSV contains all four asset weights;
- after the first submission, total allocation change is capped at 25 percentage
  points versus the previous submitted portfolio;
- weights are generated by the model, not by discretionary manual changes.

## Why the Strategy Was Changed

The earlier draft used a 45/25/20/10 portfolio as a comparison benchmark. There
is no special academic or market reason why that exact mix must anchor the final
strategy, and keeping it in the main explanation made the model look more
arbitrary than it needed to be.

The current weekly model removes that anchor. It uses a direct signal-voting
rule designed for the live competition horizon.

## Current Model

Name: **Contest-Horizon Equity-Gold Vote**

Financial rationale:

- ACWI is the main return engine in a risk-on global equity regime.
- GLD is the non-equity return engine and diversifier.
- AGG and BSV are defensive assets used when ACWI enters stress.
- Since 40% of the grade depends on June 29 live performance, the model accepts
  more equity exposure when signals are strongly positive.

Normal regime rule:

```text
ACWI gets one vote if its 3M return is higher than GLD.
ACWI gets one vote if its 6M return is higher than GLD.
ACWI gets one vote if its 200D trend gap is higher than GLD.
ACWI gets one vote if its 63D volatility is lower than GLD.

ACWI weight = 50% + 40% * ACWI_votes / 4
GLD weight  = 100% - ACWI weight
AGG weight  = 0%
BSV weight  = 0%
```

Stress regime rule:

- If ACWI is below its 200-day moving average and has negative 3-month return,
  the model enters equity stress.
- If GLD remains supportive, the model rotates toward GLD.
- If both ACWI and GLD are weak, the model rotates toward AGG and BSV.

## Current Signal Reading

Using data through 2026-05-28:

- ACWI has stronger 3-month return than GLD.
- ACWI has stronger 6-month return than GLD.
- ACWI is further above its 200-day average than GLD.
- ACWI has lower 63-day volatility than GLD.

ACWI therefore receives 4 out of 4 votes.

## Backtesting and Validation

The model was backtested with Yahoo Finance adjusted close prices from
2012-01-01 to 2026-05-28. The simulation forms weekly signals, applies the
portfolio from the following trading day, and enforces the 25 percentage point
weekly turnover limit during the historical weekly allocation path.

Two simple benchmark strategies are used:

- **Equal weight**: 25% ACWI, 25% AGG, 25% GLD, 25% BSV. This is a conservative
  diversified benchmark.
- **ACWI only**: 100% ACWI. This is an aggressive equity beta benchmark.

Long-run backtest:

| Portfolio | Annual Return | Annual Volatility | Sharpe, rf=0 | Max Drawdown | Final Wealth |
| --- | ---: | ---: | ---: | ---: | ---: |
| Strategy | 9.91% | 11.30% | 0.88 | -19.84% | 3.89 |
| Equal weight | 6.11% | 6.72% | 0.91 | -15.25% | 2.34 |
| ACWI only | 11.83% | 16.18% | 0.73 | -33.53% | 4.99 |

Interpretation:

- The strategy has higher return than equal weight because it accepts more
  growth exposure in normal markets.
- The strategy has lower return than ACWI-only, but also much lower volatility
  and drawdown.
- This is consistent with the strategy design: it is more aggressive than a
  balanced portfolio, but less concentrated than a pure equity bet.

Because the live competition lasts about one month, the model was also tested on
historical 20-trading-day windows. This validation is closer to the June
evaluation horizon than a long-run annualized statistic.

| Portfolio | Mean 20D Return | Median 20D Return | 5% Tail | 25% Quantile | Positive Window Rate |
| --- | ---: | ---: | ---: | ---: | ---: |
| Strategy | 0.88% | 1.09% | -4.60% | -0.84% | 66.20% |
| Equal weight | 0.56% | 0.51% | -2.27% | -0.56% | 63.40% |
| ACWI only | 1.00% | 1.47% | -6.17% | -1.06% | 66.67% |

Historical 20-trading-day stress and upside examples:

- Worst strategy window: -22.48%, with signal date 2020-02-20, during the
  COVID crash.
- Best strategy window: +12.62%, with signal date 2009-07-06, during the
  post-crisis rebound.

The validation shows the trade-off clearly. ACWI-only has the highest average
one-month return, but its left tail is much worse. Equal weight has the smallest
tail loss, but lower upside. The strategy sits between those two benchmarks,
which is appropriate for a competition that rewards live return but still
requires a defendable systematic model.
